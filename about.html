About Me

I'm Bolametiren "Bibi" Akinlaja, a Data Analytics Program Manager, Program Builder, and Innovation Strategist with professional experience spanning Nigeria and the United States. I design data-driven programs, systems, and partnerships that help institutions and communities translate analytics into practical action.

Rather than focusing only on data outputs, my work emphasizes building the infrastructure around data—the programs, partnerships, and learning environments that make data usable, repeatable, and measurable over time.

Building Programs and Systems That Scale

At Bowie State University's Center for Data Analytics (CDA), I lead initiatives across program design, project execution, research support, and industry engagement. My work spans both strategy and delivery—structuring programs, coordinating stakeholders, and ensuring outcomes are measurable and useful.

  • Program and project planning from concept through implementation
  • Lab operations and modernization support within the Truist DARTT Lab
  • Analytics training design that builds practical skills in analysis, modeling, and visualization
  • Applied research support through analysis, reporting, and data storytelling

Focus: building systems that teams can run, learners can grow within, and stakeholders can measure.

Founding the Data Tribe: Building a Culture of Data

A defining milestone in my work has been the creation and founding of the Data Tribe—a student-centered data community designed to provide continuity, peer leadership, and applied learning beyond the classroom.

I built the Data Tribe to close a consistent gap: students had access to tools and courses, but needed a sustained community where data felt approachable, social, and connected to real opportunities.

  • Designed the vision, structure, and operating model
  • Built engagement strategy, recruitment approach, and peer-led programming
  • Integrated professional development and industry conversations into community experiences
  • Created a welcoming entry point for students across skill levels and majors

Leadership Through Mentorship and Engagement

A consistent theme in my work is pipeline building—creating pathways that connect learners to mentors, applied projects, and professional networks. I've led engagement through structured programs and student leadership development.

Earlier, as President of the International Business Club, I organized globally focused programming and mentorship opportunities that reinforced my approach: learning is strongest when it's experiential and relational.

A Global Foundation in Strategy and Execution

Before transitioning to the U.S., I worked as a Project Manager in Nigeria within the hospitality sector, leading market research, business planning, digital optimization, and cross-functional execution.

That experience grounded my work in practical delivery—balancing constraints, building clarity, and improving performance through measurable changes.

Expanding Into AI and Strategic Decision-Making

  • Responsible and ethical AI adoption
  • AI governance and decision frameworks
  • Translating analytics into executive strategy
  • Aligning AI investments with mission-driven outcomes
