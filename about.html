About Me

I'm Bolametiren "Bibi" Akinlaja—a Data Analytics Program Manager, Program Builder, and Innovation Strategist who transforms data from numbers into opportunity. With professional experience spanning two continents, I architect data-driven ecosystems that don't just analyze information, but fundamentally reshape how institutions, communities, and individuals access and leverage analytics for transformative impact.

"I don't just work with data—I build the bridges between data and people, creating pathways where insights become action and potential becomes career-defining opportunity."

My career has been defined not by data outputs alone, but by the infrastructure I've built around them—the programs that scale, the partnerships that endure, and the learning environments where analytics becomes accessible, actionable, and transformative.

Architecting Programs That Scale Impact

At Bowie State University's Center for Data Analytics (CDA), I don't just manage programs—I orchestrate entire ecosystems of innovation. My leadership spans the full spectrum from strategic vision to tactical execution: designing program models that scale, managing complex timelines and resources, coordinating cross-functional teams across academic and industry boundaries, and ensuring every initiative delivers measurable, repeatable outcomes.

What began as project coordination has evolved into comprehensive program leadership with tangible impact:

  • Leading multi-phase analytics initiatives from concept through delivery, impacting 500+ learners and professionals
  • Spearheading the $350K+ modernization of the Truist Data Analytics Research, Trading, and Technology (DARTT) Lab—transforming it into an AI-ready innovation hub
  • Designing transformative analytics training programs that don't just teach tools, but cultivate data storytellers who can translate insights into strategic action
  • Elevating research capabilities by supporting faculty and student researchers with sophisticated analytics, compelling visualizations, and narratives that amplify their scholarly impact

As an assistant facilitator for an NSF-funded workshop on Data and the African Diaspora, I've contributed to shaping national discourse around culturally relevant data science education—ensuring that innovation in analytics reflects and serves diverse communities.

Founding the Data Tribe: Where Community Becomes Career Catalyst

Perhaps my most transformative accomplishment has been founding the Data Tribe—a pioneering student-centered data community that revolutionized how students experience analytics education. This wasn't born from a committee meeting or institutional mandate. I saw a critical gap: students had access to courses and software, but lacked the sense of belonging, peer learning, and community that transforms casual learners into confident data professionals.

I built the Data Tribe from the ground up—solo:

  • Architected the complete vision—from conceptual framework to operational model, creating a blueprint for sustainable, student-led data communities
  • Created the brand identity from scratch—designing logos, developing messaging, and crafting a recruitment strategy that resonated with students across majors and skill levels
  • Engineered a unique engagement framework that seamlessly blends technical skill-building, data storytelling workshops, and professional development experiences
  • Cultivated strategic industry partnerships bringing conversations with partners like Northrop Grumman directly to students, bridging the gap between classroom theory and career reality

The results speak to the power of community-driven learning:

  • 250+ participants engaged across workshops, industry panels, and community events
  • Established a sustainable workforce pipeline with students progressing from curious beginners to confident practitioners ready for industry roles
  • Created a welcoming, inclusive gateway into analytics—proving that data excellence thrives when learning is collaborative, human-centered, and accessible to all
  • Built a student-owned leadership engine where members don't just consume content—they create it, lead it, and shape its evolution
The Data Tribe embodies my core philosophy: data education is most powerful when it's communal, culturally relevant, and designed to empower—not just educate.

This global experience—spanning Nigeria's entrepreneurial ecosystem and the United States' innovation landscape—gives me a unique lens on program design: one that values diverse perspectives, cultural competence, and solutions that work across contexts.
