Overview

The CDA MATCH Program represents a scalable, data-driven model for experiential learning and workforce development at Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs). By strategically connecting students with faculty, staff, and industry mentors, the program transforms academic learning into applied analytics experience, fostering workforce readiness, research productivity, and long-term professional relationships. This initiative serves as a pipeline for talent development, institutional growth, and external funding opportunities.

My Role

  • Architected program framework, matching algorithms, and engagement protocols
  • Recruited and vetted 25+ student mentees across undergraduate and graduate levels
  • Engaged faculty, staff, and industry professionals as mentors
  • Coordinated 13 mentor-led research and applied analytics projects
  • Developed comprehensive onboarding materials and mentor/mentee guidelines
  • Collected and analyzed program data to measure engagement and outcomes

Objectives

  • Create a sustainable mentor-matching infrastructure connecting students with research and industry opportunities
  • Provide hands-on experience in data analytics, research methods, and professional development
  • Build cross-disciplinary collaborations spanning STEM, Business, Education, and Social Sciences
  • Establish a scalable model for workforce pipeline development at HBCUs
  • Generate measurable outcomes to attract external funding and institutional support

Approach / What I Did

  • Designed comprehensive program framework including matching criteria, engagement protocols, and success metrics
  • Launched multi-channel recruitment campaign reaching students across four college disciplines
  • Conducted individual intake meetings to assess student skills, interests, and career goals
  • Identified and recruited 15+ faculty, staff, and industry mentors with diverse research portfolios
  • Implemented strategic matching process based on research alignment, skill development needs, and career aspirations
  • Facilitated kickoff sessions, regular check-ins, and conflict resolution between mentor-mentee pairs
  • Created resource hub with templates, guides, and best practices for effective mentorship
  • Tracked program metrics including engagement rates, project completion, and participant satisfaction

Deliverables

  • Program Framework Document: Comprehensive guide outlining program structure, policies, and procedures
  • Mentor-Mentee Matching System: Strategic matching algorithm based on research interests and career goals
  • Onboarding Toolkit: Resources for mentors and mentees including expectations, communication templates, and success tips
  • 13 Active Research Projects: Ongoing mentor-led projects in data analytics, AI, social science, and business intelligence
  • Program Dashboard: Real-time tracking of engagement metrics, project status, and participant feedback
  • Impact Report: Comprehensive analysis of program outcomes, student growth, and institutional benefits

Outcomes

  • 25+ students actively engaged across undergraduate and graduate programs
  • 13 research and analytics projects launched spanning multiple disciplines
  • Cross-college representation from STEM, Business, Education, and Social Sciences
  • 15+ mentors recruited including faculty, staff, and industry professionals
  • High satisfaction rates from both mentors and mentees based on mid-program feedback
  • Scalable model ready for expansion and replication at other institutions
  • Foundation for external funding through demonstrated impact and institutional support
