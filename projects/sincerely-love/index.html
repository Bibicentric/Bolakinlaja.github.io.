Sincerely, Love Research Cohort & Mental Health Summit

Exploring emotional intelligence and mental health through data and community

Overview

Founded and managed Sincerely, Love, an innovative summer research program that employed 5 students to explore the intersection of data analytics and emotional intelligence in mental health. This initiative culminated in a community summit attracting 100+ participants for meaningful discussions about wellbeing, self-care, and the role of data in understanding mental health trends. As Program Founder and Research Director, I created a unique model that combined paid research opportunities with community impact, bridging academic inquiry and lived experiences.

My Role

  • Program founder and research director
  • Research team supervisor and mentor for 5 student researchers
  • Summit organizer and community engagement strategist
  • Curriculum designer for research training and methods
  • Budget manager coordinating student stipends and event costs

Objectives

  • Create paid research opportunities for students exploring mental health and data analytics
  • Investigate how data can inform mental health awareness and interventions
  • Build community platform for authentic conversations about wellbeing
  • Develop students' research, data analysis, and storytelling skills
  • Bridge gap between quantitative data and qualitative lived experiences

Process / What I Did

  • Designed research program structure and secured funding for 5 student stipends
  • Recruited and trained diverse student research team with various disciplinary backgrounds
  • Developed research protocols exploring mental health data, trends, and storytelling
  • Facilitated weekly research meetings with mentorship and skills development
  • Coordinated data collection efforts including surveys, interviews, and secondary research
  • Guided team in analyzing patterns and creating data visualizations
  • Planned and executed Sincerely Love Summit with panels, workshops, and networking
  • Created safe, inclusive environment for mental health discussions
  • Documented findings and developed recommendations for campus wellness initiatives

Reflection

Sincerely, Love taught me that data analytics can serve deeply human purposes when approached with empathy and cultural awareness. The challenge of researching mental health required careful attention to privacy, consent, and the emotional wellbeing of our research team. I learned to balance rigorous methodology with compassionate inquiry. The summit's success demonstrated the community's hunger for spaces that honor both data and lived experiences. Several team members have continued pursuing mental health research in graduate programs. Future iterations would include longer research timelines and stronger partnerships with campus counseling services.
