AfroTech — National Industry Engagement

Elevating HBCU data excellence through strategic partnerships and national conference leadership

Overview

Created DigiBibi, a digital storytelling and innovation brand representing Bowie State University's Center for Data Analytics (CDA) at AfroTech 2025. This initiative showcased the pipeline of data talent and cultural innovation emerging from HBCUs, positioning CDA as a leader in digital equity and AI education. As Program Manager, I conceptualized the brand identity, coordinated partnerships, and facilitated meaningful engagement with industry leaders across technology and innovation sectors.

Strategic Focus

The AfroTech initiative centered on three key strategic priorities: establishing Bowie State University's Center for Data Analytics as a national leader in HBCU data innovation, creating meaningful connections with industry partners aligned with our mission, and showcasing the exceptional talent pipeline emerging from our programs.

  • Brand Development — Developed DigiBibi brand identity including logo, messaging, and visual storytelling elements to represent CDA's innovation and cultural excellence
  • Partnership Strategy — Researched and identified key industry partners aligned with CDA's mission, creating comprehensive engagement plans for Fortune 500 technology companies
  • Student Leadership — Prepared student ambassadors with professional engagement skills, technical demonstrations, and networking strategies to represent HBCU excellence

Engagement & Presence

Our conference presence was designed to maximize visibility and create lasting connections with industry leaders. Through strategic booth positioning, professional materials, and authentic storytelling, we engaged with hundreds of technology professionals and established partnerships with leading companies.

  • Conference Strategy — Coordinated CDA's comprehensive representation at AfroTech 2025, including booth design, promotional materials, and engagement protocols
  • Industry Dialogue — Facilitated high-level partnership discussions with T-Mobile, AWS, and HPE leadership, exploring collaboration opportunities in curriculum design, student recruitment, and research initiatives
  • Network Building — Created networking opportunities for students and faculty, documenting connections for follow-up partnership development and career pathway expansion

Outcomes & Value

The AfroTech initiative delivered measurable impact for institutional visibility, partnership development, and student opportunity creation. Our presence established Bowie State University as a recognized leader in HBCU data innovation and created sustainable pathways for ongoing collaboration.
