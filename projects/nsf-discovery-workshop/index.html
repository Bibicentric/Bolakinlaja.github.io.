Overview

The NSF Discovery Workshop was a National Science Foundation-supported professional development initiative designed to strengthen data science research infrastructure and pedagogical approaches at Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs). This multi-day intensive workshop brought together faculty, researchers, and administrators to explore cutting-edge data science methodologies, collaborative research opportunities, and strategies for building sustainable data science programs that serve diverse student populations.

My Role

  • Coordinated workshop logistics including venue, materials, and technology setup
  • Managed participant registration, communications, and travel arrangements
  • Facilitated workshop sessions on data analytics tools and methodologies
  • Supported faculty in developing data science curriculum and research proposals
  • Coordinated with NSF program officers and external facilitators
  • Compiled workshop outcomes and impact assessments for NSF reporting

Objectives

  • Build capacity for data science research and education at HBCU institutions
  • Provide hands-on training in advanced analytics tools and methodologies
  • Foster collaborative networks among HBCU faculty and researchers
  • Support development of competitive NSF research proposals
  • Establish sustainable infrastructure for ongoing data science program development

Approach / What I Did

  • Designed comprehensive workshop agenda covering data science fundamentals, research methods, and grant writing
  • Recruited expert facilitators from academia, industry, and NSF program management
  • Organized hands-on sessions using real datasets and research scenarios relevant to HBCU contexts
  • Facilitated networking sessions connecting participants across institutions and disciplines
  • Created resource repository with templates, tools, and best practices for participants
  • Conducted pre- and post-workshop assessments to measure learning outcomes and satisfaction
  • Coordinated follow-up activities including mentorship circles and proposal review support
  • Documented workshop proceedings and outcomes for NSF reporting and future program improvement

Deliverables

  • Workshop Curriculum: Comprehensive training materials covering data science pedagogy and research methods
  • Resource Repository: Curated collection of tools, templates, and best practices for data science education
  • Participant Network: Connected community of HBCU faculty and researchers for ongoing collaboration
  • Grant Proposal Templates: NSF-aligned frameworks and examples for research funding applications
  • Impact Assessment Report: Comprehensive evaluation of workshop outcomes and participant feedback
  • NSF Program Report: Detailed documentation of activities, outcomes, and recommendations for program officers

Outcomes

  • 60+ educators trained including faculty, researchers, and administrators from multiple HBCUs
  • High satisfaction ratings with 95% of participants rating the workshop as excellent or very good
  • Research collaborations initiated among participants across institutions and disciplines
  • NSF proposals submitted by workshop participants leveraging learned frameworks and methodologies
  • Sustainable network established through ongoing mentorship circles and collaboration channels
  • Best practices documented for future workshops and professional development initiatives
  • Institutional capacity strengthened for data science program development at participating HBCUs

What's Next

Building on the success of this workshop, plans are underway for additional professional development sessions, virtual follow-up workshops, and a community of practice platform where participants can continue collaboration. The program is also exploring opportunities to expand the workshop model to other minority-serving institutions and develop advanced training tracks for experienced data science educators.
