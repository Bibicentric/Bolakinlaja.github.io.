Sports Analytics Camp

Inspiring the next generation through the intersection of sports and data

Overview

Co-designed and facilitated a Sports Analytics Camp in partnership with the Brian Westbrook Foundation, introducing 18 high school students to the exciting world of data analytics through their passion for sports. This hands-on program demystified data science by connecting it to real-world sports scenarios students care about, from player performance analysis to game strategy optimization. As Workshop Designer and Facilitator, I created an engaging curriculum that sparked early interest in STEM careers among underrepresented minority youth.

My Role

  • Curriculum designer for sports analytics youth program
  • Workshop facilitator and instructor for high school students
  • Partnership coordinator with Brian Westbrook Foundation
  • Hands-on project mentor guiding student analyses
  • STEM career pathway advisor connecting sports to data science

Objectives

  • Introduce high school students to data analytics and visualization concepts
  • Demonstrate practical applications of math and statistics through sports
  • Increase interest in STEM fields among minority youth
  • Build foundational data literacy skills for college and career readiness
  • Create accessible entry point to data science through familiar sports context

Process / What I Did

  • Designed age-appropriate curriculum covering data collection, analysis, and visualization
  • Created engaging activities using real sports datasets (NFL, NBA, soccer statistics)
  • Taught students to use Excel and basic data visualization tools
  • Facilitated hands-on projects where students analyzed player performance and game strategies
  • Guided teams in creating presentations of their sports analytics findings
  • Connected data concepts to career opportunities in sports, business, and technology
  • Provided one-on-one mentorship helping students troubleshoot and refine analyses
  • Celebrated student work through final presentation showcase for families

Reflection

The Sports Analytics Camp reinforced my belief that context matters tremendously in education. By framing data concepts through sports—something students were already passionate about—we overcame the intimidation factor often associated with STEM subjects. The most rewarding moment was watching a student who initially said "I'm not good at math" present a sophisticated analysis of player efficiency ratings. I learned to adapt instruction based on varying skill levels while keeping everyone engaged. Future camps would benefit from extending the program duration and incorporating more advanced tools like Python or Tableau for interested students.
