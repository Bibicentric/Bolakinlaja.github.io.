NSF Discovery Workshop: Data & the African Diaspora

Advancing culturally relevant data science education at HBCUs nationwide

Overview

Co-facilitated a National Science Foundation-funded national workshop bringing together 60+ HBCU educators to develop culturally relevant data science curricula. This collaborative initiative focused on integrating African Diaspora perspectives, histories, and datasets into data analytics education to create more inclusive and engaging learning experiences for HBCU students. As Workshop Co-Facilitator and Program Coordinator, I helped design sessions, coordinate logistics, and facilitate discussions that advanced inclusive education and cross-institutional collaboration.

My Role

  • Workshop co-facilitator and session designer
  • Program coordinator managing logistics and participant engagement
  • Discussion facilitator for curriculum development workgroups
  • Partnership liaison coordinating multi-institutional collaboration
  • Documentation lead capturing insights and recommendations

Objectives

  • Develop culturally relevant data science curricula for HBCU students
  • Build national network of HBCU data science educators
  • Identify datasets and case studies reflecting African Diaspora experiences
  • Share best practices for inclusive data analytics pedagogy
  • Create framework for ongoing cross-institutional collaboration

Process / What I Did

  • Co-designed workshop agenda and facilitation guides with NSF leadership
  • Coordinated logistics for 60+ participants including venue, technology, and materials
  • Facilitated breakout sessions exploring culturally relevant pedagogy approaches
  • Led discussions on integrating African Diaspora datasets into analytics curricula
  • Coordinated workgroups developing specific course modules and assignments
  • Captured participant insights and synthesized recommendations
  • Created resource repository of shared curricula and teaching materials
  • Developed follow-up collaboration plan for ongoing partnership

Reflection

The NSF Workshop deepened my appreciation for the power of culturally relevant pedagogy in STEM education. Witnessing educators from diverse HBCUs share innovative teaching approaches was incredibly inspiring. The challenge of coordinating 60+ busy faculty members taught me valuable project management and diplomatic skills. I learned that meaningful curriculum change requires not just resources, but sustained community and shared vision. The relationships built during this workshop have continued yielding collaborative opportunities. Future workshops would benefit from including student voices more directly in curriculum co-design processes.
