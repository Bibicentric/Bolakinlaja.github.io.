Overview

These upcoming initiatives represent the next phase of AI innovation at Bowie State University and beyond. Each project is designed to expand access to AI education, foster research excellence, and create sustainable pathways for students to engage with cutting-edge technology.

In Planning

AI Resource Hub

A centralized AI learning portal for students at Bowie State University, providing comprehensive resources for AI education and skill development.

Hub Features:

  • Tutorials - Step-by-step guides for AI tools and technologies, from beginner to advanced levels
  • Tools - Curated collection of free and accessible AI platforms, APIs, and development environments
  • Templates - Ready-to-use project templates for common AI applications (chatbots, image recognition, predictive models)
  • Responsible AI Guidelines - Ethical frameworks and best practices for AI development and deployment
  • Career Pathways - Industry insights, job descriptions, and skill maps for AI careers
  • Community Forum - Student collaboration space for AI projects and peer learning

Expected Impact:

Create a sustainable, self-service learning ecosystem that empowers students to explore AI at their own pace while maintaining high standards for ethical AI practice.

Launch Target: Summer 2025
In Development

AI Summer Research Cohort (2025)

A 6-8 week intensive research program integrating GenAI tools, applied research methodologies, and industry mentorship.

Program Structure:

  • Week 1-2: Foundations - AI fundamentals, research methods, and tool introduction
  • Week 3-4: Applied Projects - Students begin working on real-world AI research problems
  • Week 5-6: Industry Integration - Mentorship sessions with AI professionals and company visits
  • Week 7-8: Presentation & Publication - Research findings presentation and potential publication opportunities

Research Focus Areas:

  • Natural language processing and text analysis
  • Computer vision and image recognition
  • Predictive analytics for social good
  • AI ethics and bias detection
  • Generative AI applications

Student Benefits:

  • Paid research experience
  • Professional networking with industry leaders
  • Portfolio-building projects
  • Potential conference presentations
  • Graduate school preparation
Launch Target: June 2025
Proposal Stage

AI x Performing Arts (Google Pitch)

Showcasing how AI can enhance creative design, storytelling, and cultural analysis in the performing arts.

Project Goals:

  • Demonstrate AI as a creative collaborator, not a replacement for human artistry
  • Build tools for costume designers, choreographers, and directors
  • Preserve and analyze cultural performance traditions using AI
  • Create accessible AI tools for small theater companies and independent artists

Potential AI Applications:

  • Costume Design Research - AI-powered historical pattern recognition and fabric analysis
  • Script Analysis - Character development insights and narrative structure optimization
  • Movement Analysis - Computer vision for choreography development and performance improvement
  • Audience Analytics - Understanding engagement patterns and cultural impact
  • Digital Archiving - Preserving performances and making them searchable and accessible

Partnership Opportunities:

Seeking collaboration with Google Arts & Culture, local theater companies, and performing arts departments to bring this vision to life.

Proposal Submission: Spring 2025
Planning

AI Mentorship Pipeline

Pairing students with AI professionals from industry to provide guidance, career insights, and project support.

Mentorship Components:

  • One-on-One Mentorship - Monthly meetings with industry AI professionals
  • Group Workshops - Skill-building sessions on AI career paths and technical skills
  • Project Guidance - Support for student AI projects and portfolio development
  • Networking Events - Quarterly meetups with AI companies and startups
  • Internship Pathways - Direct connections to internship opportunities at partner companies

Target Industries:

  • Tech companies (Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Meta)
  • AI startups and innovation labs
  • Consulting firms with AI practices
  • Government agencies using AI
  • Healthcare and biotech AI applications

Expected Outcomes:

Increased student confidence, expanded professional networks, higher internship placement rates, and stronger preparation for AI careers.

Launch Target: Fall 2025

Innovation Pipeline Philosophy

These projects reflect a commitment to:

  • Student-Centered Design - Building programs based on student needs and aspirations
  • Industry Relevance - Ensuring skills taught match market demands
  • Ethical Foundation - Embedding responsible AI practices from the start
  • Sustainable Impact - Creating programs that continue beyond initial funding
  • Community Benefit - Extending AI education beyond campus to local communities
